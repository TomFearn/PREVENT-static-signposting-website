Welcome to Safeguard

We are your guide to the UK Government's PREVENT Scheme

The UK government's PREVENT scheme is a crucial part of the nation's counter-terrorism strategy, designed to safeguard individuals and communities from the risk of radicalisation and extremist influence. Launched as part of the larger CONTEST strategy, PREVENT aims to identify and support those at risk of engaging in harmful ideologies or violent activities, providing early interventions that foster resilience and promote integration.

On this site, you'll find accessible information about PREVENT's objectivesand the types of support available

Whether you are a professional, a parent, or a concerned citizen, this resource will help you understand how PREVENT operates, outline early warning signs to look out for and guide you to services in your local area.

Warning signs

Early warning signs of radicalisation
    Isolating from Family and Friends
  • Withdrawal from usual social circles or becoming secretive about activities and relationships.
    • Expressing Extremist Views
  • Advocating or supporting ideologies that promote hate, violence, or intolerance, often with rigid or polarized beliefs.
    • Increased Intolerance
  • Showing strong dislike or hostility toward specific groups, often based on race, religion, nationality, or political beliefs.
    • Emotional Instability
  • Displaying sudden emotional changes, anger, or distress, particularly when discussing specific issues or events.
    • Growing Interest in Conspiracy Theories
  • Engaging with or sharing conspiracy theories, particularly those related to government, religion, or social issues, which may encourage a worldview based on fear or mistrust.
    • Seeking or Sharing Extremist Content
  • Consuming, creating, or spreading material online or in-person that promotes radical or violent ideas, especially through social media.
    • Questioning Mainstream Beliefs
  • Rejecting accepted societal norms or questioning democratic values, often with an increasing focus on the "us versus them" mentality.
    • Desire to Travel to Conflict Zones
  • Expressing a strong interest in traveling to areas where extremist groups are active, or showing unusual curiosity about regions with high levels of conflict.
    • Sudden Change in Appearance or Behavior
  • Sudden Change in Appearance or Behavior - Altering dress, habits, or language to align with specific ideological groups, sometimes accompanied by a marked change in lifestyle or moral values.
    • Expressing Feelings of Injustice or Victimhood
  • A growing sense of alienation, feeling misunderstood, or having a victim mindset that may be used to justify violent or extremist ideas.

Local Services

Gloucestershire Unity Network

The Gloucestershire Unity Network works with local schools, youth centers, and community organizations to foster a sense of belonging and inclusion among young people. GUN provides workshops, mentoring, and after-school programs aimed at promoting resilience against extremist influences.

Travel there now

Community Watch Gloucestershire

Community Watch Gloucestershire is a volunteer-led initiative focused on keeping communities safe by promoting awareness of radicalization warning signs. CWG organizes public seminars and provides an anonymous hotline for concerned residents to report worrying behaviors they observe.

Travel there now

Safe Spaces Gloucestershire

Safe Spaces Gloucestershire is a drop-in support center for individuals of all ages who feel vulnerable to extremist recruitment or who need guidance on countering radical messaging. The center offers free counseling, mental health support, and educational resources to empower individuals with tools to resist extremism.

Travel there now

Youth Empowerment Initiative

The Youth Empowerment Initiative aims to empower young people in Gloucestershire with the skills and confidence to challenge extremist narratives. YEI runs creative workshops, leadership programs, and social media campaigns that encourage critical thinking and community involvement among youth.
